The Link Rot Problem in Scholarly Communication

Digital scholarship depends on stable references, but the web's mutability undermines this assumption. As documented by Zittrain et al., over 70% of the URLs provided in articles published in the Harvard Law Review did not lead to originally cited information. This rate of decay accelerates over time, raising questions about the long-term integrity of the scholarly record.

The problem extends beyond traditional scholarship. The Columbia Journalism Review found that eight major AI search tools provided incorrect or inaccurate answers to more than 60% of 1,600 test queries, with error rates ranging across tools and query types. When AI systems themselves cannot reliably retrieve factual information, the citation chain breaks at both ends.
